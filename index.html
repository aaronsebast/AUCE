Tourist Attractions

Auce Old Town: Explore cobblestone streets, traditional Latvian architecture, and quaint cafés offering local delicacies.

Auce Manor: This 19th-century manor is surrounded by lush gardens, ideal for history lovers and photography enthusiasts.

Nature Parks: Several parks around Auce offer trails, wildlife observation, and scenic spots for family outings.

How to Reach Auce

Auce is well-connected by road and rail. Here’s how you can reach:

  • By Car: A scenic drive from Riga takes approximately 1.5 hours. Ample parking is available in and around the town.
  • By Train: Rail connections from Riga to Jelgava, followed by a local service to Auce, provide a hassle-free travel experience.
  • By Bus: Regular buses operate between major Latvian cities and Auce, ensuring affordability and convenience.
  • By Air: For international visitors, Riga International Airport is the nearest major airport, followed by a drive or train journey to Auce.

Travel Time

From Riga: 1.5 hours by car or approximately 2 hours by train and bus.

From Vilnius: Around 3 hours by car, crossing the Latvian border into Zemgale region.

From Tallinn: A longer trip of approximately 4.5 hours by car or train, offering a glimpse of the Baltic countryside.

Weather

Auce enjoys a temperate climate with four distinct seasons:

  • Spring: The blooming landscapes and mild temperatures (10°C-15°C) make it ideal for hiking and outdoor photography.
  • Summer: Sunny and warm, with temperatures ranging between 20°C and 25°C. Perfect for river activities and local festivals.
  • Autumn: Golden leaves and cooler days (10°C-15°C) create a picturesque setting for nature walks and cultural events.
  • Winter: Snow-covered streets and freezing temperatures (-5°C to -15°C) make it perfect for cozy stays and holiday festivities.

Culture

Auce is a vibrant center of Latvian traditions, blending folklore with modern culture:

  • Experience Jāņi, the traditional Midsummer festival, featuring bonfires, singing, and dancing.
  • Visit local art galleries and artisan markets showcasing traditional crafts like weaving and pottery.
  • Enjoy community theatre performances and folk music concerts that highlight Latvia’s artistic heritage.

History of Auce

The town of Auce has a rich and layered history:

  • Medieval Era: Auce began as a small trading post within the Duchy of Courland and Semigallia.
  • 18th-19th Centuries: The town flourished during the industrial age, with significant contributions to agriculture and trade.
  • 20th Century: Auce played a pivotal role during Latvia’s fight for independence and endured Soviet occupation.

Food & Music

Discover the culinary and musical delights of Auce:

  • Local Cuisine: Try dishes like grey peas with bacon, wild game, and black bread desserts.
  • Music Festivals: Annual events like the Folk Music Festival and Modern Rock Nights attract visitors from across Latvia.
  • Instruments: Listen to traditional Latvian music featuring instruments like kokle, alongside contemporary performances.

Geography

Auce is situated in the fertile plains of the Zemgale region:

  • Known for its rolling hills, lush forests, and proximity to the scenic Lielupe River.
  • Surrounding landscapes support agriculture, with vibrant fields of rye and barley.
  • Several nature reserves and parks offer trails for hiking, cycling, and birdwatching.

Events & Festivals

Auce hosts various festivals year-round:

  • Spring Festival: Celebrating the arrival of spring with flower markets and music.
  • Harvest Festival: An autumn celebration of local produce and folk traditions.
  • Winter Fair: Featuring Christmas markets, ice skating, and festive light displays.

Economy

The town’s economy is primarily driven by:

  • Agriculture: Known for dairy farming, crop cultivation, and eco-tourism.
  • Small Enterprises: Local crafts, food production, and family-owned businesses.
  • Tourism: A growing sector due to cultural and natural attractions.

Education

Auce offers a range of educational opportunities:

  • Several primary and secondary schools providing quality education.
  • Vocational programs specializing in agriculture and craftsmanship.
  • Access to higher education institutions in nearby cities like Jelgava and Riga.

Accommodation

Visitors can choose from:

  • Cozy guesthouses offering authentic Latvian hospitality.
  • Hotels and B&Bs in the town center for comfortable stays.
  • Farm stays for a unique rural experience.

Nature & Outdoor Activities

Auce’s natural beauty is perfect for outdoor enthusiasts:

  • Explore hiking trails through lush forests and scenic hills.
  • Enjoy water activities like kayaking and fishing on the Lielupe River.
  • Visit nearby nature reserves for birdwatching and wildlife spotting.
