History of Auce

Auce has a rich historical background dating back to the 15th century. This town was originally part of the Duchy of Courland and Semigallia and later came under Russian Empire rule. In 1920, Latvia gained independence, and Auce became a part of the Latvian state. Its historical buildings and local architecture reflect the town’s diverse past, blending Latvian and Baltic German influences.

Geography

Located in southern Latvia, Auce is surrounded by scenic landscapes with flat farmlands, small forests, and natural rivers. The nearby Lielupe River flows through the area, providing picturesque spots ideal for nature lovers. Auce's position near the Lithuanian border also makes it an interesting place for cross-border visitors.

How to Reach Auce

By Car: Drive about 1.5 hours from Riga on the A9 highway.

By Train: Take a train from Riga to Jelgava, then transfer to Auce.

By Bus: Buses are available from Riga, offering a comfortable ride with countryside views.

Tourist Attractions

  • Auce Old Town: Explore historical buildings and charming streets.
  • Auce Manor: A beautiful manor with gardens and a tranquil environment.
  • The Lielupe River: Ideal for walks, bird-watching, and picnics by the water.
  • Local Museums: Discover the town's history and cultural heritage through small museums.

Weather

Auce has a temperate climate with four distinct seasons. Summers are mild, with temperatures ranging from 15°C to 25°C. Winters can be cold and snowy, with temperatures often dropping below 0°C. Spring and autumn bring moderate temperatures, making these ideal seasons for visiting.

Culture

The culture in Auce is a blend of Latvian and German influences. Traditional Latvian festivals, such as Jāņi (Midsummer), are widely celebrated with folk music and dance. Locals also participate in seasonal harvest festivals and craft fairs. The town maintains traditional customs and encourages cultural preservation.

Food & Music

Auce offers traditional Latvian dishes, including grey peas with bacon and rye bread. These dishes are hearty and flavorful, often featuring locally sourced ingredients. Music in Auce is an integral part of local life, with folk music played during festivals and gatherings. The kokle, a traditional Latvian instrument, is often used to perform beautiful melodies unique to Latvia.
