History of Auce

Auce's history dates back to the 15th century when it was part of the Duchy of Courland and Semigallia. Over centuries, it transitioned under various powers, including the Russian Empire and independent Latvia in the 20th century. The town retains its historical charm with buildings reflecting its Baltic and Latvian heritage.

Geography

Auce is located in the Zemgale region, known for its fertile lands and natural beauty. The town is surrounded by flat farmlands, small forests, and the picturesque Lielupe River. Its proximity to the Lithuanian border adds to its geographic appeal for visitors.

How to Reach Auce

By Car: Drive via the A9 highway from Riga to reach Auce in about 1.5 hours.

By Train: Take a train from Riga to Jelgava, then transfer to a connecting train or bus to Auce.

By Bus: Direct buses from Riga to Auce offer a comfortable and scenic journey.

Tourist Attractions

  • Auce Old Town: Stroll through historical streets and admire traditional architecture.
  • Auce Manor: Visit this historic estate with beautiful gardens.
  • The Lielupe River: Enjoy serene walks or picnics along this scenic river.
  • Local Museums: Explore small but rich museums that celebrate Auce's culture and history.

Weather

Auce has a temperate climate. Summers are warm with temperatures ranging from 15°C to 25°C, while winters are cold and snowy, often below 0°C. Spring and autumn provide mild and pleasant conditions for visitors.

Culture

Auce’s cultural heritage is a blend of Latvian and Baltic traditions. Local festivals such as Midsummer celebrations, folk music performances, and craft fairs showcase the town’s rich history and vibrant community spirit.

Food & Music

Enjoy traditional Latvian cuisine, including hearty dishes like grey peas with bacon and freshly baked rye bread. Music plays a vital role in local culture, with folk songs and the use of the traditional kokle instrument during festivals and events.

Travel Time

From Riga: 1.5 hours by car or approximately 2 hours by train and bus.

From Vilnius: About 3 hours by car, making Auce a convenient destination for travelers from Lithuania.
