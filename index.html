Tourist Attractions

Auce Old Town: Explore cobblestone streets, traditional Latvian architecture, and quaint cafés offering local delicacies.

Auce Manor: This 19th-century manor is surrounded by lush gardens, ideal for history lovers and photography enthusiasts.

Nature Parks: Several parks around Auce offer trails, wildlife observation, and scenic spots for family outings.

How to Reach Auce

Auce is accessible by various modes of transportation:

  • By Car: A scenic 1.5-hour drive from Riga on the A9 highway.
  • By Train: Take a train from Riga to Jelgava, then connect to Auce via bus or train.
  • By Bus: Regular buses operate between Riga and Auce, offering a budget-friendly option.

Travel Time

From Riga: 1.5 hours by car or approximately 2 hours by train and bus.

From Vilnius: A 3-hour journey by car, making it easily accessible for cross-border visitors.

Weather

Auce enjoys a temperate climate with distinct seasons. Summers are warm (15°C-25°C), perfect for exploring. Winters are snowy, with temperatures often dropping below freezing. Spring and autumn offer mild weather, ideal for sightseeing.

Culture

Auce is a hub of Latvian traditions, from folklore to modern festivities. Experience traditional Midsummer celebrations, immerse in folk music, and explore craftsmanship passed down through generations.

History of Auce

Auce's origins trace back to medieval times when it was part of the Duchy of Courland and Semigallia. Its rich heritage includes centuries of trade, agriculture, and cultural exchange. The town endured through occupations and independence struggles, shaping its unique identity.

Food & Music

Indulge in local cuisine, including hearty soups, smoked meats, and rye bread. Music is central to Auce's identity, with folk instruments like the kokle and contemporary performances making every event special.

Geography

Auce is situated in the fertile plains of the Zemgale region:

  • Known for its rolling hills, lush forests, and proximity to the scenic Lielupe River.
  • Surrounding landscapes support agriculture, with vibrant fields of rye and barley.
  • Several nature reserves and parks offer trails for hiking, cycling, and birdwatching.
