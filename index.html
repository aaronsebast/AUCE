Tourist Attractions

Auce Old Town: Explore cobblestone streets, traditional Latvian architecture, and quaint cafés offering local delicacies.

Auce Manor: This 19th-century manor is surrounded by lush gardens, ideal for history lovers and photography enthusiasts.

Nature Parks: Several parks around Auce offer trails, wildlife observation, and scenic spots for family outings.

How to Reach Auce

Auce is well-connected by road and rail. Here’s how you can reach:

  • By Car: A scenic drive from Riga takes approximately 1.5 hours. Ample parking is available in and around the town.
  • By Train: Rail connections from Riga to Jelgava, followed by a local service to Auce, provide a hassle-free travel experience.
  • By Bus: Regular buses operate between major Latvian cities and Auce, ensuring affordability and convenience.
  • By Air: For international visitors, Riga International Airport is the nearest major airport, followed by a drive or train journey to Auce.

Travel Time

From Riga: 1.5 hours by car or approximately 2 hours by train and bus.

From Vilnius: Around 3 hours by car, crossing the Latvian border into Zemgale region.

From Tallinn: A longer trip of approximately 4.5 hours by car or train, offering a glimpse of the Baltic countryside.

Weather

Auce enjoys a temperate climate with four distinct seasons:

  • Spring: The blooming landscapes and mild temperatures (10°C-15°C) make it ideal for hiking and outdoor photography.
  • Summer: Sunny and warm, with temperatures ranging between 20°C and 25°C. Perfect for river activities and local festivals.
  • Autumn: Golden leaves and cooler days (10°C-15°C) create a picturesque setting for nature walks and cultural events.
  • Winter: Snow-covered landscapes, with temperatures ranging from -5°C to -10°C, offer opportunities for winter sports and a cozy holiday atmosphere.

Culture

Auce is a vibrant community where Latvian traditions meet modern living. Local art galleries, theater performances, and traditional dance exhibitions offer a rich cultural experience.

History of Auce

Founded in the 13th century, Auce has a rich history steeped in Latvian heritage. The town has witnessed centuries of evolution, from medieval fortifications to a modern-day town filled with historical sites.

Food & Music

Latvian cuisine is rich with hearty flavors. Try the famous piragi (filled pastries), grey peas with bacon, and enjoy local folk music festivals.

Geography

Auce is located in the Zemgale region, surrounded by lush forests, rivers, and small lakes. Its scenic landscapes provide a haven for nature lovers.

Events & Festivals

Auce hosts several annual festivals such as the Auce Folk Festival and the Summer Carnival, offering visitors a blend of local music, art, and cuisine.

Economy

The economy of Auce is largely based on agriculture, small industries, and tourism, with many local businesses focusing on organic farming and artisanal goods.

Education

Auce has several primary and secondary schools, offering quality education for children. The town is also home to vocational schools, fostering skilled trades.

Accommodation

Visitors can choose from a range of accommodations, from cozy guesthouses to modern hotels, ensuring a comfortable stay in Auce.

Nature & Outdoor Activities

The surrounding forests and rivers provide a perfect setting for hiking, kayaking, and cycling. Wildlife enthusiasts will enjoy birdwatching in the nearby nature reserves.
